Grigori Yakovlevich Perelman

Grigori Perelman is a Russian mathematician famous for solving the Poincare Conjecture, an extremely complex topological problem that had eluded the best minds for over a century. There was an assigned prize of $1,000,000 for anyone who succeeds to provide a solution for this mathematical problem. However, Grigori Yakovlevich refused to accept any kind of praise or money for his achievement. This situation led to many interviews (that he refused to give) and questions such as "Why?"and "How?". Mr Perelman was never the person to accept any prize, or praise on that matter, for his discoveries. In one of his rare interviews he said: "The prize was completely irrelevant for me. Everybody understood that if the proof is correct, then no other recognition is needed." "I'm not interested in money or fame."

I not only admire his intellect and determination for solving complex mathematics problems, but the fact that no matter how important his discovery was, he never accepted fame or praise. Discoveries are made not to become famous but to bring something new to the world. If you have the ability to bring something meaningful into this world then why not use it? It is indeed your choice to make profit off of it but initially the ability to accomplish something others might struggle with is a gift. You might call Perelman selfless or weird but he lives for mathematics, he breathes it, and he is satisfied. So, it is nobody's place to judge his life choices. I, as a mathematician myself, support his decision and admire him for being able to stay himself even after being persuaded to accept the reward. Sometimes, doing something just because you like it, is wonderful and is something I want to be able to do.
